About

La Nuova Cucina delle Specialità Regionali by Vittorio Agnetti is a testament to Italy’s rich and varied culinary heritage. Published in 1909 and reprinted unchanged in 1913, it is recognized as the first truly comprehensive collection of regional Italian recipes, offering an authentic snapshot of Italy’s diverse gastronomic traditions.

Unlike earlier works that featured regional dishes, Agnetti was the first to systematically organize them into a cohesive and structured volume, meticulously dividing recipes by region. His approach was unique—not relying solely on written sources, but drawing directly from oral traditions, personal encounters, and firsthand experience across Italy.

What makes this book particularly valuable is its ability to capture both the simplicity of humble, everyday dishes and the sophistication of elaborate, refined preparations. Agnetti’s work bridges the gap between tradition and the evolving landscape of Italian cuisine. A true ethnographic record, it remains an invaluable resource for those who seek to understand and preserve Italy’s culinary heritage.

The Author

Vittorio Agnetti was a pioneering figure in Italian gastronomy, whose work laid the foundation for the systematic study of regional cuisine. Published in 1909, his La Nuova Cucina delle Specialità Regionali was the first cookbook to comprehensively organize traditional Italian recipes by region, preserving local flavors and culinary heritage.

Unlike his predecessors, Agnetti relied not only on written sources but also on firsthand research, gathering recipes directly from the people who prepared them. His work captures the essence of both humble peasant dishes and refined bourgeois cuisine, offering a rich and authentic portrait of Italy’s gastronomic diversity.

More than just a collection of recipes, Agnetti’s book remains a vital document of Italy’s culinary past, proving that food is a living expression of history, culture, and identity.

Agnetti's Variations of Recipes

Vittorio Agnetti took a meticulous yet dynamic approach to Italian cuisine, preserving regional traditions while recognizing the natural evolution of recipes. La Nuova Cucina delle Specialità Regionali documented authentic dishes as they were prepared across Italy, capturing both time-honored techniques and local adaptations. His work reflects the richness of Italy’s culinary heritage, balancing respect for tradition with an understanding that food is a living, evolving expression of culture.
